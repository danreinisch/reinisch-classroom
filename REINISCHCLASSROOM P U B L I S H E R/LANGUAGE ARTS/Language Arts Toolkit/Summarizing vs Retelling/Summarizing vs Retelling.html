📖 What is Retelling?

🔄 Retelling = Replay Button

Retelling means sharing ALL the events and details from a story in the same order they happened. It's like pressing replay on a movie!

📚 Original Story:

"The Busy Morning"

Jake woke up late because his alarm didn't go off. He quickly brushed his teeth and threw on yesterday's clothes. He grabbed a banana from the kitchen and ran to catch the school bus. The bus driver, Mrs. Garcia, waved at him as he climbed aboard. Jake sat next to his friend Alex and shared his banana. They talked about their math test until they arrived at school. Jake realized he forgot his backpack and had to call his mom to bring it. She arrived just before first period started.

📖 A Good Retelling:

First, Jake woke up late because his alarm didn't work. Next, he quickly got ready by brushing his teeth and putting on yesterday's clothes. Then, he grabbed a banana and ran to catch the bus. When he got on the bus, Mrs. Garcia waved at him and he sat with his friend Alex. They shared the banana and talked about their math test during the ride. After they got to school, Jake realized he forgot his backpack. Finally, his mom brought it just before first period started.

Word count: ~85 words (close to original length)

Good Retelling Includes:

  • All the main events in correct order
  • Important details and characters
  • Sequence words (first, next, then, finally)
  • Most of the information from the original

🎯 When to Use Retelling:

  • Comprehension check: "Tell me what happened in the story"
  • Sharing with friends: "You missed the movie - here's what happened"
  • Study guides: Making sure you remember all the events

📋 What is Summarizing?

🎯 Summarizing = Highlight Reel

Summarizing means sharing ONLY the most important information - the main idea and key supporting details. It's like watching highlight clips instead of the whole game!

📚 Same Original Story:

"The Busy Morning"

Jake woke up late because his alarm didn't go off. He quickly brushed his teeth and threw on yesterday's clothes. He grabbed a banana from the kitchen and ran to catch the school bus. The bus driver, Mrs. Garcia, waved at him as he climbed aboard. Jake sat next to his friend Alex and shared his banana. They talked about their math test until they arrived at school. Jake realized he forgot his backpack and had to call his mom to bring it. She arrived just before first period started.

📋 A Good Summary:

Jake had a chaotic morning when he woke up late and had to rush to get ready for school. He managed to catch the bus but then realized he had forgotten his backpack, so his mom had to bring it to school.

Word count: ~35 words (much shorter than original)

🔽 The Summary Funnel

📚 ALL the details and events
⬇️
🎯 Important information only
⬇️
✨ Main idea + key details

Good Summary Includes:

  • Main idea or central message
  • Only the most important details
  • Much shorter than the original
  • Written in your own words

🎯 When to Use Summarizing:

  • Book reports: "What was this book about?"
  • News articles: Getting the key facts quickly
  • Study notes: Capturing essential information for tests

⚖️ Side-by-Side Comparison

Let's see the key differences between retelling and summarizing:

📖 RETELLING

  • Length: Almost as long as original
  • Details: Includes most/all details
  • Order: Same order as original
  • Purpose: Show you understood everything
  • Focus: Sequence of events

📋 SUMMARIZING

  • Length: Much shorter than original
  • Details: Only the most important ones
  • Order: May reorganize for clarity
  • Purpose: Give overview of main points
  • Focus: Main idea and key themes

🔍 The Information Filter Strategy

When summarizing, you need to act like a filter - keeping the important stuff and letting the details flow through!

⚡ The 3-Step Filter Process

1 CAPTURE: What's the main idea? (The big picture)
2 COLLECT: What are 2-3 key supporting details?
3 CUT: What details can you leave out?

🧪 Filter in Action - Science Article Example:

Original Text:

"Scientists at Stanford University conducted a groundbreaking study on sleep patterns in teenagers. Dr. Sarah Chen and her team of 12 researchers spent three years studying 500 high school students from five different schools in California. They discovered that teenagers naturally fall asleep later and wake up later than adults due to changes in their circadian rhythms during puberty. The study, published in the Journal of Sleep Research on March 15th, found that teens need 8-10 hours of sleep per night but most only get 6-7 hours. This sleep deficit affects their academic performance, mood, and physical health. The researchers recommend that high schools start classes no earlier than 8:30 AM to better align with teen sleep cycles."

🎯 CAPTURE Main Idea:

Teenagers have different sleep patterns than adults due to biological changes

📌 COLLECT Key Details:

  • Teens need 8-10 hours of sleep
  • Most only get 6-7 hours
  • Affects academic performance and health

✂️ CUT Extra Details:

  • Dr. Chen's name
  • 12 researchers
  • Publication date
  • Journal name

✨ Final Summary:

Result: A Stanford study found that teenagers naturally have different sleep patterns than adults and need 8-10 hours of sleep per night, but most teens only get 6-7 hours, which negatively affects their academic performance and health.

Original: 150 words → Summary: 35 words

🤔 When to Use Each Method

Knowing WHEN to retell vs WHEN to summarize is just as important as knowing HOW!

📖 Use RETELLING When:

  • Teacher asks "What happened in the story?"
  • You need to show you read and understood everything
  • Sharing a story with someone who missed it
  • Studying the sequence of events for a test
  • The assignment specifically asks for a retelling
  • You're practicing reading comprehension

📋 Use SUMMARIZING When:

  • Writing a book report or review
  • Someone asks "What was it about?"
  • Creating study notes for a test
  • You have limited time or space
  • Comparing multiple texts or ideas
  • Writing an introduction or conclusion

🎯 Real-World Examples:

📖 Retelling Situations:

  • Friend asks: "I missed the episode - what happened?"
  • Teacher says: "Tell me about the story we read"
  • Parent asks: "How was your day at school?"

📋 Summary Situations:

  • Friend asks: "Is that movie worth watching?"
  • Teacher says: "What's the main theme of this book?"
  • Parent asks: "What did you learn in science today?"

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

Even experienced readers make these mistakes! Let's learn to spot and fix them.

❌ Mistake #1: Summary Too Long

Problem: "The summary is almost as long as the original text."

Why it happens: Including too many details instead of focusing on main ideas.

Fix: Ask "Is this detail essential to understanding the main point?"

❌ Mistake #2: Missing the Main Idea

Problem: "The summary has details but no clear main point."

Why it happens: Getting caught up in interesting details instead of the big picture.

Fix: Start with "This story/article is mainly about..."

❌ Mistake #3: Retelling When You Should Summarize

Problem: "First this happened, then this, then this..." (when asked for main points)

Why it happens: Not reading the directions carefully or understanding the purpose.

Fix: Always check: Am I supposed to tell everything or just the key points?

❌ Mistake #4: Adding Your Own Opinions

Problem: "This boring story was about..." or "I think the character should have..."

Why it happens: Mixing personal reactions with objective summary.

Fix: Stick to what actually happened - save opinions for analysis questions.

Summary Quality Check

  • Is it much shorter than the original?
  • Does it include the main idea?
  • Are only the most important details included?
  • Is it written in my own words?
  • Does it make sense on its own?

Retelling Quality Check

  • Are events in the correct order?
  • Did I include all major events?
  • Are important characters mentioned?
  • Did I use sequence words (first, next, then)?
  • Would someone understand the whole story?

🏆 Pro Tips to Avoid Mistakes:

  • Read the instructions twice - Look for key words like "summarize," "retell," "main idea"
  • Set a word limit - Summaries should be about 1/4 the length of the original
  • Check your purpose - Are you showing comprehension or giving an overview?
  • Read it aloud - Does it capture the essence without unnecessary details?
