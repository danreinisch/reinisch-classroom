Parts of Speech

An Interactive Guide to Understanding the Building Blocks of Language

Navigate through different word types and learn their definitions, examples, identification tests, and usage in sentences.

Nouns

Definition

A noun is a word that names a person, place, thing, or idea. Nouns are the subjects and objects in sentences and form the backbone of most communication.

Examples

teacher city book happiness dog freedom

How to Identify

Ask yourself: "Can I put 'the', 'a', or 'an' in front of this word?" If yes, it's likely a noun. Also try: "Can this word be made plural?" Most nouns can be.

Sentence Example

The teacher read a book about friendship to her students.

Verbs

Definition

A verb is a word that expresses an action, occurrence, or state of being. Verbs tell us what the subject is doing or what is happening to the subject.

Examples

run think is became dance exist

How to Identify

Ask: "What is the subject doing?" or "What is happening?" The answer is usually a verb. Also try adding "-ing" to the word - if it makes sense, it's likely a verb.

Sentence Example

The children laughed loudly and played in the park while their parents watched.

Adjectives

Definition

An adjective is a word that describes or modifies a noun or pronoun. Adjectives provide more information about the qualities, characteristics, or attributes of the thing being described.

Examples

beautiful tall red happy ancient delicious

How to Identify

Ask: "What kind?" or "Which one?" or "How many?" The answer is often an adjective. Try putting the word between "very" and a noun - if it makes sense, it's likely an adjective.

Sentence Example

The brave knight rode his white horse through the dark forest.

Adverbs

Definition

An adverb is a word that modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb. Adverbs often describe how, when, where, or to what extent something happens.

Examples

quickly very yesterday carefully here extremely

How to Identify

Ask: "How?" "When?" "Where?" or "To what extent?" Many adverbs end in "-ly". Try removing the word - if the sentence still makes sense but loses detail about the action, it's likely an adverb.

Sentence Example

She carefully and slowly walked upstairs yesterday.

Pronouns

Definition

A pronoun is a word that takes the place of a noun. Pronouns help avoid repetition and make sentences flow more smoothly by referring back to nouns mentioned earlier.

Examples

he she it they this who

How to Identify

Ask: "What noun could this word replace?" If you can substitute a specific noun for the word and the sentence still makes sense, it's likely a pronoun.

Sentence Example

Sarah loves books. She reads them every day because they make her happy.

Prepositions

Definition

A preposition is a word that shows the relationship between a noun (or pronoun) and other words in a sentence. Prepositions often indicate location, direction, time, or manner.

Examples

on in under before with through

How to Identify

Think of a box. Ask: "Where is something in relation to the box?" Words like "in," "on," "under," "beside" are prepositions. Prepositions are usually followed by a noun or pronoun.

Sentence Example

The cat jumped onto the table and knocked the vase off during the party.

Conjunctions

Definition

A conjunction is a word that connects words, phrases, or clauses. Conjunctions help create compound sentences and show relationships between ideas.

Examples

and but or because although while

How to Identify

Ask: "Does this word join two parts of a sentence?" or "Does it show the relationship between two ideas?" Try removing it - if the sentence breaks into two separate thoughts, it's likely a conjunction.

Sentence Example

I wanted to go to the movies, but it was raining, so I stayed home and read a book instead.

Interjections

Definition

An interjection is a word or phrase that expresses strong emotion or sudden feeling. Interjections are often followed by exclamation points and stand alone or are set off by commas.

Examples

wow! ouch! hooray! oh no! alas yikes!

How to Identify

Ask: "Does this word express strong emotion?" or "Could this word stand alone as an exclamation?" Interjections often come with exclamation points and express feelings like surprise, joy, or pain.

Sentence Example

Wow! That was an amazing performance. Bravo! You should be proud of yourself.

Articles

Definition

Articles are special types of adjectives that come before nouns to indicate whether the noun is specific or general. There are only three articles in English.

Examples

the a an

How to Identify

There are only three articles: "the" (definite), "a" and "an" (indefinite). "The" refers to something specific, while "a" and "an" refer to something general. Use "an" before vowel sounds.

Sentence Example

The student brought a pencil and an eraser to the exam.
