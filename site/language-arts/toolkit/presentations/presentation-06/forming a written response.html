üìã The Response Structure

1. Topic Sentence

2. Supporting Details

3. Conclusion

Restates the prompt and summarizes your response

üéØ The Topic Sentence

Purpose:

The topic sentence restates the question/prompt in your own words and presents your main argument or position.

Example Prompt:

"What are the benefits of reading for at least 30 minutes daily?"

Strong Topic Sentence:

"Reading for thirty minutes each day provides numerous benefits including improved vocabulary, enhanced critical thinking skills, and reduced stress levels."

Weak Topic Sentence:

"Reading is good for you because it helps in many ways."

Topic Sentence Checklist:

  • Restates the question/prompt in different words
  • Clearly states your main position
  • Previews what your supporting details will cover
  • Is specific and focused

üîç Supporting Details with Evidence

You need at least 3 supporting details, each backed by evidence. Evidence gives your arguments credibility and strength.

Types of Evidence:

üìä Statistics & Data

Numbers, percentages, research findings

üë®‚Äçüî¨ Expert Opinions

Quotes from authorities, professionals

üìö Research Studies

üìñ Examples

Real-world cases, historical events

üîó Logical Reasoning

Cause-and-effect relationships

üí≠ Personal Experience

Observations, anecdotes (when appropriate)

Example Supporting Detail:

Detail 1: Daily reading significantly improves vocabulary acquisition. Evidence: According to research by Nagy & Herman (1987), students who read for 30 minutes daily learn approximately 1,000 new words per year through context clues alone.

üîç Supporting Details Examples

Continuing with our reading example, here are additional supporting details with evidence:

Detail 2: Regular reading enhances critical thinking and analytical skills. Evidence: A Stanford University study found that students who engaged in sustained reading showed 23% improvement in analytical reasoning tests compared to those who didn't read regularly.
Detail 3: Daily reading serves as an effective stress-reduction technique. Evidence: Dr. David Lewis's research at the University of Sussex demonstrated that reading for just 6 minutes can reduce stress levels by up to 68%, making it more effective than listening to music or having a cup of tea.

Supporting Details Checklist:

  • Each detail directly supports your topic sentence
  • Evidence is specific and credible
  • Details are arranged in logical order
  • Transitions connect ideas smoothly
  • You have at least 3 strong supporting points

üéØ The Conclusion

Your conclusion should restate the writing prompt and summarize how your evidence supports your main argument.

Strong Conclusion Example:

"In conclusion, the question of whether daily reading for thirty minutes provides benefits is clearly answered through the evidence presented. The research demonstrates that consistent reading habits lead to expanded vocabulary, stronger analytical thinking, and improved mental well-being, making it a valuable daily practice for anyone seeking personal growth."

Weak Conclusion Example:

"In conclusion, reading is really good and everyone should do it."

Conclusion Checklist:

  • Restates the original question/prompt
  • Summarizes your main supporting points
  • Reinforces your position with confidence
  • Provides closure without introducing new ideas
  • Leaves a lasting impression on the reader

Conclusion: In conclusion, the question of whether daily reading for thirty minutes provides benefits is clearly answered through the evidence presented. The research demonstrates that consistent reading habits lead to expanded vocabulary, stronger analytical thinking, and improved mental well-being, making it a valuable daily practice for anyone seeking personal growth.
