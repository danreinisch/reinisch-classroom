🤔 What Are Prefixes and Suffixes?

🔤 Word Parts That Change Meaning

Prefixes and suffixes are like building blocks that we add to root words to create new meanings!

📍 PREFIX

Goes BEFORE the root word

RE
PLAY

RE + PLAY = REPLAY
(to play again)

📍 SUFFIX

Goes AFTER the root word

PLAY
ER

PLAY + ER = PLAYER
(one who plays)

💡 Memory Tip:

PREfix = PRE (before) • SUFfix = comes after (sounds like "after")

📍 Common Prefixes

Prefixes change the meaning of the root word. Here are some you use every day!

Prefix
Meaning
Examples

🎯 Prefix in Action:

DIS
AGREE

DIS (not) + AGREE = DISAGREE (to not agree)

Suffixes often change the part of speech or add specific meanings to words.

💡 Tips and Tricks

🎯 When You See a Long Word:

  • Don't panic!
  • Look for familiar word parts
  • Cover up prefixes and suffixes
  • Find the root word first
  • Build the meaning piece by piece

🧠 Memory Helpers:

  • UN- usually means "not"
  • RE- usually means "again"
  • -ER usually means "one who"
  • -FUL usually means "full of"
  • -LESS usually means "without"
