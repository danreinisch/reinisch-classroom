🔍 Let's become nutrition detectives!

🏷️ What is a Nutrition Label?

A nutrition label is like a "report card" for food!

It tells us:

⚡

How much energy (calories) the food gives us

🍎

What nutrients are inside

📏

How much we should eat (serving size)

🧪

What ingredients were used to make it

💡 Think of it as: A food's ID card that tells us everything we need to know!

🔍 Where Do We Find Nutrition Labels?

🥫 On packaged foods:

  • Back or side of cereal boxes
  • Side of canned foods
  • Back of snack packages
  • Side of bread bags
  • On yogurt containers

🚫 Foods that DON'T have labels:

  • Fresh fruits and vegetables
  • Fresh meat from the deli
  • Fresh bread from the bakery

🎯 Your Mission Today:

At home, find 3 different food packages and look for their nutrition labels!

🌟 Why Are Nutrition Labels Important?

💪

Stay Healthy

Know what nutrients your body is getting

⚖️

Control Portions

Learn how much is the right amount to eat

🔄

Compare Foods

Choose between similar products

🚫

Avoid Allergens

Check ingredients for things you can't eat

Real Life Example:

If you want to choose between two breakfast cereals, the nutrition labels help you pick the one with less sugar and more fiber!

🧩 Parts of a Nutrition Label

Nutrition Facts

8 servings per container

Serving size: 1 cup (240ml)

Calories 150

Total Fat 3g

Sodium 470mg

Total Carbs 22g

Protein 8g

Ingredients: Water, Tomatoes, Carrots, Salt...

1. Serving Size: How much equals one serving
2. Calories: Energy the food gives you
3. Nutrients: The good and not-so-good stuff
4. Ingredients: What's actually in the food

📏 Understanding Serving Size

🎯 Serving Size = The Foundation of Everything!

ALL the numbers on the label are based on ONE serving size.

8 servings per container

Serving size: 1 cup

Calories 500

Worksheet Question Practice:

Q: What is the serving size for this product?

A: 1 cup

Q: How many servings are in the container?

A: 8 servings

💡 Important Rule:

If you eat 2 cups instead of 1, you need to DOUBLE all the numbers!

⚡ Understanding Calories

What are calories?

Calories = Energy units that fuel your body

Think of them like gasoline for a car!

🔋

Low Calories

Under 100 per serving

Examples: Most vegetables, some fruits

⚡

Moderate Calories

100-400 per serving

Examples: Bread, yogurt, most meals

🔥

High Calories

Over 400 per serving

Examples: Ice cream, fried foods

🎯

Daily Goal

About 2000 calories total per day for most adults

Worksheet Question Practice:

Q: How many calories per serving?

A: Look for the big bold "Calories" number

📋 Reading Ingredients Lists

🔢 The Secret Order:

Ingredients are listed from MOST to LEAST amount!

Ingredients: Whole Grain Oats, Corn Starch, Sugar, Salt, Tripotassium Phosphate, Vitamin E Added to Preserve Freshness

🕵️ What this tells us:

  • Most: Whole Grain Oats (good!)
  • Second: Corn Starch
  • Third: Sugar
  • Least: Vitamin E (just a tiny bit for freshness)

Worksheet Question Practice:

Q: Look up Tripotassium Phosphate. What is it?

A: It's a food additive that helps prevent clumping and acts as a preservative

Q: What other uses does it have?

A: Used in cleaning products and as a fertilizer

⚠️ Understanding Added Sugars & Trans Fat

🍯

Added Sugars

Sugar added during processing

Goal: Less than 50g per day

🚫

Trans Fat

Unhealthy fat to avoid

Goal: 0g (zero grams!)

Sugars 41g

Added Sugars 10g

Trans Fat 0g ✓

Worksheet Question Practice:

Q: Does this product contain added sugars?

A: Yes, 10g of added sugars

Q: Does this product contain trans fat?

A: No, 0g trans fat

🧂 Understanding Sodium & Making Choices

What is Sodium?

Sodium = Salt. Too much can raise blood pressure.

Daily Goal: Less than 2,300mg per day

Sodium Guidelines:

  • Low: 140mg or less per serving
  • Medium: 140-400mg per serving
  • High: Over 400mg per serving

Worksheet Question Practice:

Q: How much sodium is in one serving of this product?

A: 180mg (look at the nutrition facts)

Q: Would you eat this food? Why or why not?

A: This is a personal choice! Consider the calories, nutrients, ingredients, and how it fits into your daily eating.

📅 Understanding Expiration Dates

🔍 Different types of dates on food:

✅

"Best By"

Food is freshest before this date

Still safe to eat after!

⚠️

"Use By"

Use before this date for best quality

Don't use much after

🛑

"Expires"

Don't eat after this date

Especially important for dairy/meat

🥛

"Sell By"

Store guideline

Usually safe 3-5 days after

🛒 Shopping Tip:

Always check dates before buying. Choose items with the latest dates if you're planning to use them later in the week.

🏠 At Home Tip:

Put newer items in the back, older items in front. Use the older items first!

🧠 Advanced Shopping Strategies

🕒 Best Times to Shop:

Early morning or late evening for fewer crowds. Wednesday-Thursday for fresh sales.

🛒 Cart Strategy:

Get a cart even for small trips. Carrying too much leads to forgetting items or dropping things.

❄️ Temperature Strategy:

Shop frozen/dairy last. These items stay fresh longer when picked up at the end.

📱 Technology Helps:

Use your phone for shopping list apps, calculator for price comparison, store apps for deals.

🎯 Stay Focused:

Stick to your list but be flexible if you find a great deal on something you regularly use.

💳 Payment Ready:

Have your payment method ready at checkout. Organize your bags efficiently.
